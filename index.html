The Core Collection

Each object below uses real published data and its own scientifically-justified mapping to sound. Click into any of them for the full data, the audio, and the reasoning behind it.

Variable Star

Mira

A star that swings between bright and dim over a 332-day cycle — sonified as a melody, one note per real day of observation.

 Neutron Star

The Crab Pulsar

The crushed core of a star that exploded in 1054 CE, spinning ~30 times a second — sonified as its real rhythm.

 Exoplanet

Kepler-10 b

A rocky world orbiting its star every 20 hours — sonified as a steady tone with a real, periodic transit dip.

 Comet

Halley's Comet

A 76-year orbit calculated from real orbital mechanics — sonified as a journey from silence to a brilliant, brief peak.

About

Why this project exists

I love astronomy, and I love music — I often have music playing while I study space. Watching NASA's own data sonification work inspired me to try building something like it myself: taking real astronomical measurements and honestly translating them into sound, rather than just making something that sounds nice.

The full story — the data sources, the reasoning behind each mapping, and the problems I ran into along the way — is documented in the project journal on GitHub.

Data: AAVSO · ATNF Pulsar Catalogue · NASA Exoplanet Archive · JPL Small-Body Database
